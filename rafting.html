Whitewater Rafting Trip Lead
U.S. National Whitewater Center

Between May 2020 and May 2022, I seasonally worked as a raft guide at the U.S. National Whitewater Center, the world's largest artificial whitewater river. Don't let the term "artificial" fool you, these rapids were just as intense as the wild; this is where tryouts are held for the Olympics. Halfway through my first season I was promoted to Trip Lead, which put me in charge of the flotilla of rafts running each trip.

I am extremely proud of my time as a guide; it taught me confidence and resilience during a time when I had none. Guiding people through an intimidating experience and seeing their fear turn to joy is the most rewarding feeling in the world.

While I ultimately had to move on, rafting has given me strength under pressure and the confidence needed to succeed in chaotic environments.

I had a lot of unique crews; here are some of my favorites:

  • Four super buff construction workers. Fully utilizing their power, I got them absolutely soaked and they were giggling like kids.
  • A crew consisting of one Australian, two Scots, one Englishman, and one Chicagoan. They were a hoot.
  • A hispanic family of whom only one spoke English. Instead of making her translate the entire time, I had them teach me how to say the rafting commands in Spanish; we had a blast.
  • Bachelor/bachelorette parties were always fun. I even had one sweet sixteen.
  • One family who came several times and would always request me. It was awesome to see the kids' confidence grow every time. (They're front row in the video below)
  • A deaf man; it honestly wasn't a big challenge and he was a super strong paddler, but it was still rewarding to help increase accessibility of the sport.
